Personal Notes:
Richard Wood – Will summary
PRC 28/17/267
made 16 Aug 1631 proved 1 Sep 1631
• Richard Wood of Maidstone, woolen draper
• to the poor people of the parish of Leeds, Kent 40 shillings 1 month after decease
• to uncle & master William Wood £20
• to aunt Jane Wood, wife of William, £10
• to William Wood son of uncle William 40 shillings
• to Uncle William’s 4 daughters, Jane Ann, Francis & Mary, £5 apiece
• to Mathew Wood, son of uncle Mathew Wood of Canterbury 20 shillings
• to Christopher Wood son of uncle Mathew Wood 20 shillings
• to sister Jane Wood £20
• to sister Ann Clarke, wife of William Clarke of Harrettisham 20 shillings
• to sister Elizabeth Curlinge, wife of John Curlinge of St Laurence, 20 shillings
• to brother-in-law John Curlinge Thanet the residue of all goods, chattells, ready money
• brother-in-law John Curlinge to be executor
• to executor John Curlinge, for his own proper use, all rents of estate due for next Michaelmas
• to 3 sisters Elizabeth Curlinge, Ann Clarke and Jane Wood and their heirs, messuage, 2 barnes, stable powderhouse [?], garden, orchard & 123 acres of land in the parish of St Laurence, Thanet now in the tenure of John Curling
Witnesses Jo[hn] Pope, John Entwicke

The will establishes that his sister Elizabeth married John Curling of St Laurence. He had two other sisters: Ann married to William Clarke and Jane (spinster?). He had an uncle William Wood married to Jane who had a son William, another uncle Mathew Wood who had a son Christopher.
